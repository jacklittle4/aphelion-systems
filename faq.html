FAQ

Answers before the first call.

The common questions are usually about time, ownership, content, hosting, and whether the project actually needs custom software.

How long does a project take?

Starter sites usually take 5-7 days. Business websites often take 1-2 weeks. Interface prototypes take 2-3 weeks, and custom systems usually start around 3-6 weeks depending on complexity.

Do I own the code?

Yes. Once the project is paid for, the finished project files belong to you. Aphelion can also help deploy them and explain how updates work.

What do I need to provide?

A rough description of the business, preferred contact method, any existing logo or brand assets, page content if you have it, and examples of sites or apps you like.

Can I update the site later?

Yes. Simple sites can be structured for easy edits, and care plans are available when you want help with updates, fixes, or future improvements.

Do I need a custom app?

Not always. If a polished website solves the problem, keep it lean. A custom app makes sense when you need accounts, dashboards, data, admin workflows, automations, or integrations.

Can you host it?

Aphelion can help set up hosting on platforms like GitHub Pages, Netlify, Vercel, or another host that fits the project.

Do I need a paid domain right away?

No. A project can launch for free on GitHub Pages first. A custom domain can be added later when the business is ready to pay for one.

How do payments and deposits work?

Small projects can be handled with a simple agreed payment schedule. Larger projects usually work best with a deposit before build and the remaining balance before final handoff.

How many revisions are included?

Scope should include a clear revision pass for content, layout, mobile behavior, and launch details. Extra rounds or major scope changes can be quoted.

What happens after launch?

The launch checklist covers mobile review, forms, metadata, sitemap, deployment, and handoff notes. A care plan can cover ongoing updates.

Can you set up forms, payments, and analytics?

Yes. Free or low-cost tools can support contact forms, payment links, Search Console readiness, and analytics once the client has the right accounts.

Will the site be mobile-friendly and accessible?

Yes. Responsive layout, readable spacing, labels, alt text, keyboard-friendly navigation, and clear CTAs are part of the launch review.

Still unsure?

Send the rough version.

You do not need a perfect brief. A current website, goal, budget range, and timeline are enough to start.
