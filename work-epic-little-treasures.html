Epic Little Treasures

A handmade-goods online storefront I designed, built, and launched end to end for a real client. Product catalog, payments, orders, and a custom admin she runs herself.

Real client · shippedE-commerce

A live storefront, not a template

Epic Little Treasures is a small handmade-goods business. The owner needed a real online store she could keep selling from and manage on her own, without paying a monthly platform fee for features she would never use. I built it, launched it, and handed it over.

View the live site →

The goal

Give a handmade-goods business a professional storefront it fully owns. It needed to show products clearly, take orders and payments without a heavy checkout system, let customers get in touch, and stay easy for the owner to update herself. The whole thing had to work well on a phone, since that is where most of her customers find her.

What I built

I handled design, build, data, and launch as one project, so nothing got lost in a handoff.

  • A product catalog with photos, descriptions, and pricing, laid out to read cleanly on phones and desktops.
  • Buy-now payment links on each product, so customers can pay without a complicated checkout to maintain.
  • Order and status badges that make the state of an order clear at a glance.
  • Contact forms so customers can ask about custom pieces and reach the owner directly.
  • A custom admin the owner manages herself, to add products, update details, and keep the store current without calling me.
  • Responsive, mobile-first pages and a deployment set up so the site runs reliably.

The result

Epic Little Treasures is live at epiclittletreasures.com. It is a complete storefront the owner runs day to day on her own, from a real build rather than a rented template. She can list new products, take payments, and answer customer questions without a developer in the loop, and the code and accounts belong to her.

This was Aphelion's first shipped client project, and it is the clearest example of what the studio does: design, build, data, and hosting handled end to end by one person, priced fairly, and left in the client's hands at launch.
